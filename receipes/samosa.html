SAMOSA

Description

A samosa is a fried or baked pastry that's typically triangular or cone-shaped and filled with spiced vegetables, meat, or fish. Samosas are a popular snack in many parts of the world, including India, the Middle East, and East Africa

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, green beans), boiled
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped (optional)
  • 1 tbsp ginger, grated or finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp amchur (dry mango powder) or lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)
  • 1-2 tbsp oil (for sautéing)

For the Samosa Pastry:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup oil or ghee
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Water (as needed to form dough)

For Frying:

  • Oil (for deep frying)

Steps

1. Prepare the Filling:

  1. Heat 1-2 tbsp oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions, green chilies, ginger, and garlic. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  3. Add turmeric powder, garam masala, coriander powder, and red chili powder. Stir well.
  4. Add the boiled mixed vegetables and mashed potatoes. Mix thoroughly and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and amchur (or lemon juice). Mix well and cook for another minute.
  6. Add fresh cilantro, mix, and remove from heat. Let it cool.

2. Prepare the Samosa Dough:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, salt, and oil (or ghee).
  2. Gradually add water and knead into a smooth dough. The dough should be firm but pliable.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.

3. Shape the Samosas:

  1. Divide the dough into small equal-sized balls.
  2. Roll each ball into a thin oval or round disc (about 4-5 inches in diameter).
  3. Cut the disc in half to make two semi-circles.
  4. Form a cone by folding each semi-circle in half. Seal the edge with a little water to hold the shape.
  5. Fill the cone with the prepared potato and vegetable filling.
  6. Pinch the open edge together to seal the samosa, forming a triangular shape.

4. Fry the Samosas:

  1. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  2. Carefully drop the samosas into the hot oil, a few at a time, without overcrowding the pan.
  3. Fry until they turn golden brown and crispy, about 4-5 minutes.
  4. Remove the samosas with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.

5. Serve:

Serve hot with tamarind chutney or mint chutney. Enjoy your homemade samosas!
